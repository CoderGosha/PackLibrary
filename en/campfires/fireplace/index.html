Reflector fire (fireplace lay)

A fire with "automatic" wood feed — it lets you cook and keep warm at the same time without manually feeding it. Popular with hunters and anglers for burning a long time and, if built right, for being reusable. It's usually paired with a heat reflector — an angled wall made from a tarp, a survival blanket, or branches and spruce boughs, the same way it's done for a nodya.

  1. Set up two parallel poles angled away from the fire — these act as the rails for the logs.
  2. Lay two parallel base logs on the ground and light a fire between them.
  3. Stack smaller-to-larger logs on top of the angled rails, forming something like the back of a chair.
  4. Drive stakes in on the sides so the stacked logs can't roll off.
  5. As the base logs burn down, the stacked logs will roll down and feed the fire automatically.
  6. Set the reflector up behind the fire and sleep no closer than about half a meter from it, making sure smoke doesn't build up under the shelter.
