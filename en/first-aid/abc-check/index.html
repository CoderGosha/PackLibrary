Consciousness, breathing and CPR

Once the scene is safe, check whether the victim is conscious and breathing — that decides whether you start CPR.

  1. Check for consciousness — speak to the victim and gently shake their shoulders.
  2. If they're conscious, move straight to the secondary injury check.
  3. If they're unconscious, check breathing first, not pulse — under stress it's easy to mistake your own pulse for the victim's.
  4. Clear the chest of clothing, gently tilt the head back (one hand on the forehead, two fingers under the chin), then for a few seconds listen with your ear, feel with your cheek, and watch the chest move.
  5. If they're breathing, roll them into the recovery position and move to the secondary check.
  6. If there's no breathing and no consciousness, start CPR immediately: 30 chest compressions 5–6 cm deep, alternated with 2 rescue breaths.
  7. Keep going until signs of life appear or rescuers arrive — outdoors this can take hours rather than minutes, so switch with another person when you can and don't give up early.
