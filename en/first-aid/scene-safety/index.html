Scene assessment and safety

The first step before doing anything else: make sure helping doesn't create a second casualty.

  1. Look around and assess real dangers near the victim — fire, water, a cliff edge, avalanche risk, electric current, moving vehicles.
  2. Don't rush in while the danger is still active — remove it first (put out the fire, cut the power) or wait for it to pass.
  3. If waiting is too risky and the danger can't be removed, evacuate the victim from the hazard zone right away, even by dragging them by their clothes or legs — don't wait for ideal conditions.
  4. Remember the rescuer's golden rule: the number of casualties must not grow once you start helping — risking yourself is only justified if it's the only way to save the other person.
