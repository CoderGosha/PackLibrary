Bowline

A fixed loop at the end of a rope. Unlike the figure-eight, it stays easy to untie even after being loaded hard. Used to tie onto a tree, an anchor, or as a rescue loop. Always needs a stopper knot on the tail.

  1. Make a small loop in the rope, away from the end.
  2. Bring the working end up through that loop from underneath.
  3. Wrap the working end around the standing part and bring it back to the loop.
  4. Feed the working end back down through the same loop.
  5. Tighten, and finish with a stopper knot on the tail.
