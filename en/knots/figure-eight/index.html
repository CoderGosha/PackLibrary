Figure-eight knot

A simple, reliable knot. Used as a safety and stopper knot to keep a rope from slipping out of a carabiner or buckle. Its main downside: it cinches under load, so it's harder to untie after a hard fall than a bowline.

  1. Fold the rope and form a loop in the working end.
  2. Wrap the working end around the standing part of the rope.
  3. Thread the working end back through the loop.
  4. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends snug.
